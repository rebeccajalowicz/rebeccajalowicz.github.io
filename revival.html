Revival

Revival is a concept of a game developed in a 24 hour hackathon taking place at The College of New Jersey. It is an open world game in which the user wakes up in a house and needs to complete tasks in order to make a mask and go outside into a polluted world. From there on is it an open world game where the player needs to work to clean up the world from the pollution which is poisoning it.

The Problem:

Create a game that simulates a form of quarantine and relies on familiar feelings from living through the covid-19 pandemic, but acts as an escape from the anxieties that comes from the pandemic.

Research



Our group was both inspired by how the pandemic affects people's mental health but also how games relate to that as a type of coping mechanism. These ideas came from a group discussion from our own personal experiences and were validated through research on the topics. First Iteration:

I started the design process by making a quick graphic in illustrator of what the world would look like ideally. It was a simple map just to give the team an idea of what we wanted and how to proceed with developing the game. From here we decided that with the time constraints, it was best to focus on the house and a small portion of the outside world.

Paper Prototyping

After this I began making paper prototypes for how the game would look. Initially we were planning having the house be a square and the outside be square as well. In order to make the game in unity, there would have to be separate images for the background and furniture in order to make the furniture colliders so the main character could not walk over an object like a bed or a table.

While my role was to make the background whereas another team member made the furniture, add smaller details and design the main character, lovingly named Timmy.

The pitch:
After waking up from a horrifying nightmare that is their harsh reality, the player must escape their house in order to cleanse the world from pollution. Before doing so, they must build a mask to protect themselves from the polluted air and to freely traverse the open world, starting off with their own neighborhood. From picking up garbage to taking out forest fires, the player must do whatever it is possible to reduce pollution so they can save their beloved planet from the harmful and poisonous takeover.

Future Considerations

This was a 24 hour event and while I am very proud of the outcome and all of the hard work my team and I put into this there are still some things I would love to see if the project ever gets continued.

What to continue in terms of game development:

  • Fully fleshed out world
  • Make games for all of the tasks needed to move on within the world itself
  • Make the game multiplayer, be able to save the world with your friends!

Along with this the UX designer of the team, while we were able to map user journeys and the entire project was based around a user‚Äôs mental health in the time of a pandemic, I wish i was able to validate some of the ideas we came up with. There was just simply not enough time to reach out to people in order to test our ideas and game and we were not able to go out into the field to test either as we are still very restricted during the pandemic. However if we had the time and we were able to do user testing here is what I would have liked to do:

  • Pre-Development:
    Survey people what games they like the most right now, if it helps them cope, what they need to feel comforted/wind down these days, is climate change/pollution too real of a topic for escapism? Would that make you more depressed?
  • During Development:
    Run a cognitive walkthrough, make sure the user knows when a task needs to happen and validate if they are able to complete the task.
  • Post-Development:
    Have the users play the game and interview them afterwards. Some examples of questions might be: Is this too triggering/too real? (the purpose is to bring joy not frustration), Is the game fun? Would you play with your friends? Do you feel a sense of escapism when you play? What other changes would you recommend?

Then I would simply ideate and iterate based off of all of the research found at each step in order to make this game the best it could possibly be.
