HALL MILLS MURDER MYSTERY ⋁

Hall Mills Murder Mystery

This site was based off an article found in Trentonia (history collection found in the Trenton Public Library) explaining the Hall Mills Murder case. It's purpose is to inform the user of the contents of the article in an interesting and engaging format. Users go through evidence and "solve" one of the longest unsolved murder mysteries.

This was a group project for the class IMM 280 Design Perspectives. My group and I worked with Trentoniana as a client to make this website for visitors to use to educate themselves about the Hall Mills Case. We worked with the directors in order to give them a website that works best for their typical visitors while still being an entertaining form of learning. Our project can also be viewed on the Trentoniana website found here.

My Role: Wordpress Developer
   ~ My group split responsibilities in 4 ways: writing, video editing, graphics and the developing of the website itself (my role).
   ~ Essentially I was given all of the content needed for the site and put it all together cohesively. Specifically I chose the theme, plugins and overall development of all of the pages on the site along with planning how the "game" aspect of the project was going to work.
   ~ For the home and explore page, all that was necessary was to put in all the information required of those pages as those were static, imformative pages.
   ~ The game page consists of several pages on the site to give the illusion of flipping through a report file.
   ~ Within the pages of the game, I used a plugin for pop ups so that the user can click on pictures and evidence without exiting the game itself. On the very last page of the game I used a plug in to create a poll so users can vote on who they think commited the crime. This is how users "solve" the unsolved mystery.

250TH ANNIVERSARY OF THE SETTLEMENT OF TRENTON ⋁

250th Anniversary of the Settlement of Trenton

This site was based off an magazine publication titled "The 250TH Anniversary of the Settlement of Trenton," found in Trentonia (history collection found in the Trenton Public Library). It's purpose is to act as the voice of the publication as if it were able to speak. The project in question was to find an artifact in Trentoniana and create a project in which to portray if the object could speak, what would it say?

This was a solo project for the class IMM 280 Design Perspectives.I worked with Trentoniana as a client to make this website for visitors to use to educate themselves about the anniversary of the settlement of Trenton.

My interpretation of this assignment was to create a blog where the magazine would "speak," so to say. The magazine had blog posts about different topics about Trenton both from the past and now. I chose to host this website on wordpress because wordpress already has a good template for blogs.

My Role: Since this was a solo project, I did everything this website is composed of. I chose a blank theme, did my own layout and made all of the graphics featured throughout. I used mostly pictures of the publication itself throughout the website. However, I decided to make graphics for the blog page itself because it made the page more playful as a whole. Since the blogs are from a pretend narrator, having a cartoon version of the magazine added to whole "if it could talk" theme of the project.

AZG APO ⋁

AZG APO

For my Fall 2019 Semster in college I was the Webmaster for Alpha Phi Omega (co-ed community service fraternity) on the executive board. Unfortunately the server the database our old website that we had previously used was deleted just before I was elected. The entirety of my semester was spent developing a new site for APO to use.

My Role: I developed the wordpress aspect of this site. There is a calendar portion to this website utilized by the brotherhood that was made by someone else. Since that semester I have moved on to another role within the brotherhood therefore the new webmaster has been updating the website as well.

Specifically I was in charge of the initial creation of the website. I bought the domain, and pointed it to the wordpress site and calendar. Along with this I designed the basic theme and layout of the website. This includes the color scheme and the general layout. Since that semester there have been updates to the website but I contributed significantly to the creation and early development of this site along with the look and feel of the site as a whole.
